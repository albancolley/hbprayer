Herne Bay & Villages Prayer Guide

Beltinge, Broomfield, Greenhill, Hampton, Herne, Herne Bay, Hoath, Marshside and Reculver

"Rejoice always, pray continually, give thanks in all circumstances; for this is God's will for you in Christ Jesus"
1 Thessalonians 5:16-18

Thursday
1 April		 Maundy Thursday and we pray for our Queen who again this year will be unable to distribute Maundy money in person. We thank God for her continued Christian witness and her tireless devotion to duty. Pray for a time of peace for the Royal Family.
Friday
2 April		 Remembering all the churches in our area who will be streaming Good Friday services today, we pray that technology will be free from any problems. Pray for the 10am Messy Good Friday Church from St Bartholomew’s via Zoom.
Saturday
3 April		 Today is Holy Saturday, a time of waiting and, for the early disciples, a time of despair. Pray that all those who are grieving the loss of loved ones during this past year may be comforted. Pray for safety for tomorrow’s live Easter celebrations in our churches.
Sunday
4 April		 The angel said to the women “Do not be afraid for I know that you are looking for Jesus, who was crucified. He is not here; he has risen just as he said. Come and see the place where he lay”
Matthew 28:5,6

HE IS RISEN – HE IS RISEN INDEED!
Monday
5 April		 As we look forward to greater freedom from the past lockdowns, we pray that people will have learned lessons and will be wise about following rules about masks and social distancing so that the government’s 4 month ‘Road Map’ will be possible.
Tuesday
6 April		 We think of our policemen and women and pray that the recent violence and destructive behaviour at protest Apriles will stop and that the evil ways of those causing damage to life and property will be changed.
Wednesday
7 April		 ‘We must learn to love them both – those whose opinions we share and those whose opinions we reject, for both have laboured in the search for the truth and have helped us in finding it’ so wrote Thomas Aquinas (1225-1274) We pray today for tolerance.
Thursday
8 April		 We remember in our prayers business leaders who have to make tough decisions about staffing when starting up again – decisions that will affect lives and livelihoods. Pray for a spirit of kindness to prevail as we all seek to help where we can.
Friday
9 April		 Volunteers at food Banks and food deliveries across this area work hard to meet the continuing needs of local families who struggle to feed their families. Pray that our supermarkets will offer continued support and that the volunteers will be kept safe.
Saturday
10 April		 Saturdays are always busy days for our local supermarkets and so today we pray for safety for all those who have worked tirelessly to keep the shelves full during the past year. Pray that any abusive behaviour to staff will be dealt with swiftly and firmly.
Sunday
11 April		 May the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace as you trust in him, so that you may overflow with hope by the power of the Holy Spirit.
Romans 15:13
Monday
12 April		 Give thanks for the scientists who continue to closely monitor changes to the virus and research a range of vaccines to control the devastating spread of Covid 19 across the world. Pray that, with God’s grace, we might be freed from this devastating plague.
Tuesday
13 April		 Pray today for our young children. Many have been growing up knowing only masks and separation from family members and friends. Pray that these experiences will not cause damage and that a better way of living and mixing will soon be possible.
Wednesday
14 April		 Pray for people of all ages suffering from mental illnesses resulting from the pandemic restrictions. Ask our Lord to bring healing and peace to so many troubled and tortured souls and make us sensitive and patient to help where we are able.
Thursday
15 April		 We continue to pray for our NHS workers as they now face the huge task of trying to catch up with operations and treatments that were delayed by Covid 19. Pray for all those people who have been waiting many months for surgery.
Friday
16 April		 We continue to pray for our church leaders as they face a future where worship will inevitably be different. Many churches will be facing financial difficulties and possibly reduced congregations as confidence in returning to church may take time for some.
Saturday
17 April		 Pray for a greater understanding about Climate Change. May governments across the world accept responsibility for the profligate way many are living and make wise and informed decisions to bring about major changes to protect our planet for the future.
Sunday
18 April		 Look around you. Everything you see is God’s – the heavens above and beyond, the Earth and everything on it.
Deuteronomy 10:14
Monday
19 April		 As schools go back after the Easter holiday, we pray for our teachers and classroom assistants who will still be working through all the extra challenges that this pandemic has brought. Give thanks for the online teaching they have done during the past year.
Tuesday
20 April		 We pray for HB Salvation Army and for those faithful Christians who look after many care homes in the town with weekly news sheets offering bible stories and nature notes. May they be blessed and soon be able to visit at last.
Wednesday
21 April		 Our prayers today are for more restrictions to be eased. People of all ages are struggling to cope with loneliness and boredom. We remember especially those who haven’t had a real hug from grandchildren for over a year.
Thursday
22 April		 Support groups are a lifeline to many struggling with addictions and not being able to meet regularly has had devastating consequences for many. We pray for better support networks and freedom from life draining dependencies.
Friday
23 April		 Give thanks for our local radio stations. Radio Kent and Radio Cabin. Pray for the presenters who have kept us informed with news and phone ins. With fun and music they have kept us afloat and been great company to the lonely.
Saturday
24 April		 As we enjoy the uplifting beauty and green shoots and flowers of Spring, we pray for rebirth for our nation. May the Holy Spirit move in power across our land and bring more and more people into the Kingdom this year.
Sunday
25 April		 I will pour out my Spirit on all people. Your sons and daughters will prophesy, your old men will dream dreams, your young men will see visions.
Joel 2:28
Monday
26 April		 We pray for all those working in the catering industry – for restaurants, pubs and cafes. As we meet up with friends once again in gardens we pray for safety and joyous reunions for many
Tuesday
27 April		 We are aware that many businesses have failed during lockdown and that hundreds have lost their jobs so we pray for real growth in employment opportunities in this area. Pray for families who cannot see the way ahead, may they be supported and helped.
Wednesday
28 April		 Pray for our Community Wardens as they continue to patrol our areas, collecting prescriptions, offering help with shopping and generally supporting all those who need help. Pray for their safety.
Thursday
29 April		 Pray for those who are planning to get married in the coming months. Pray for all who will be celebrating Silver, Golden and Diamond weddings this year. Pray too for those struggling with relationships, may they find peace and love.
Friday
30 April		 Looking ahead, prepare the way with our own prayers for the planned Pentecost National Day of Prayer on May 23rd. Pray that wonderful changes will take place as a result of this exciting initiative.
